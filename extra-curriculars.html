Model United Nations

Model United Nations has been one of the most formative experiences of my journey. It has refined my ability to communicate with clarity and conviction, strengthened leadership skills, and fostered confidence in high-pressure discussions. Above all, MUN has instilled a habit of solution-oriented thinking — approaching complex global challenges through analysis, collaboration, and purposeful dialogue rather than debate alone - without any intent of solving problem.

Conferences Attended

  1. DPSBMUN '24

    DPSBMUN '24 was my first MUN conference hosted by Delhi Public School, Bareilly and it remains deeply special to me. I represented Italy in the UN Security Council and it introduced me to the true essence of diplomacy and debate. The 2-day conference was very formative and sparked a journey that I continue to value deeply.

  2. JSBCMUN '24

    JSBCMUN ’24 - hosted by Jaipuria School Bansal Campus, Lucknow - was a challenging yet enriching experience. Representing Lebanon in the UN Human Rights Council pushed me to engage with complex issues and demanding discussions. The difficulty of the portfolio tested my preparation and adaptability, making the experience deeply instructive and instrumental in my growth as a delegate.

  3. DPSB INTRAMUN '24

    DPSB INTRAMUN ’24 was a refreshing and enjoyable experience. Representing the UAE in the UN Security Council allowed me to engage more freely and confidently in committee sessions. It taught me the importance of collaboration in conferences.

  4. DPSBMUN '25

    DPSBMUN ’25 marked a turning point in my MUN journey. Representing Russia in the UNO on Drugs and Crime, and being part of the Organising Committee, made the experience especially engaging. I got to meet some great people in debating world. The conference led to noticeable growth in my debating skills, and earning a Verbal Mention made the experience even more rewarding.

  5. JSBCMUN '25

    JSBCMUN ’25 was a challenging yet enriching experience. Representing the United States in the UN Commission on the Status of Women, on the agenda surrounding Roe v. Wade, pushed me to engage deeply with complex and sensitive issues. The conference strengthened my ability to craft effective speeches and express arguments with clarity. Receiving a Verbal Mention offered meaningful validation and marked another step forward in my growth as a delegate.
